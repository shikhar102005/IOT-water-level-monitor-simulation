Hardware Breakdown
COMPONENTS
USED
01
NodeMCU
Microcontroller

This little board does most of the heavy lifting. It reads the sensor, decides whether to kick the pump on or off, and ships data up to the cloud — all thanks to the built-in Wi-Fi that makes wiring so much simpler.

ESP8266 80 MHz Wi-Fi 802.11b/g/n 11 GPIO
02
HC-SR04
Ultrasonic Sensor

Sits at the top of the tank and bounces ultrasonic pulses off the water surface. The time it takes for the echo to return tells you exactly how far down the water is — no contact with the water needed.

2–400 cm range ±3mm accuracy 5V VCC 40 kHz
03
5V Relay
Actuator Module

Acts as the go-between for the microcontroller and the pump. The NodeMCU outputs a tiny signal, the relay translates that into switching actual mains power — keeping your 3.3V board safely away from the pump circuit.

5V trigger 10A @ 250V Optocoupled NO/NC
04
Blynk IoT
Cloud Platform

Handles the cloud side of things — receives readings from the NodeMCU, stores them, and pushes everything to the mobile app in real time. Setting up the dashboard took under an hour, which honestly surprised us.

MQTT / HTTP Mobile App Real-time Notifications
05
Water Pump
Output Actuator

The one physical output of the whole system. When the tank runs low, the relay clicks and the pump starts filling. Once it hits the upper mark, it shuts right back off — no human needed anywhere in that loop.

12V DC Submersible Auto control
06
Dashboard
User Interface

Where you actually see what's happening. The gauge updates live, the history chart shows how the level has moved, and there's a manual override if you want to take control. Alerts come through even when the app is closed.

iOS / Android Web Dashboard Alerts Manual Override

System Workflow
HOW IT WORKS
01
◈
Measure Distance

The HC-SR04 sends out a short ultrasonic burst and waits for it to bounce back. That echo time is all you need — divide by two for the one-way distance.

02
◉
Calculate Level

Subtract the measured gap from the total tank height and you have the water level. A simple rolling average takes care of any noisy or jittery readings.

03
⬡
Threshold Logic

Too low — pump on. Too high — pump off. A small buffer zone between the two limits stops the relay from flickering every few seconds.

04
◆
Publish via MQTT

Every reading gets packaged and sent up to Blynk over Wi-Fi. The whole round-trip typically clears in under 50ms — fast enough that the dashboard feels genuinely live.

05
◎
User Notified

The gauge on the app updates, a notification pings if something needs attention, and you can step in to take manual control whenever you like — from anywhere.
